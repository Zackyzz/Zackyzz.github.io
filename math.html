Beautiful Integrals

The first time that I saw Feynman's trick I was in awe, to be honest that was also the time that I started loving integrals. In order to describe the trick it's easier to just show an integral that can be solved by that method. We'll start with the following integral: $$I_1=\int_0^1 \frac{x-1}{\ln x}dx$$

Mainly the idea is to introduce a new parameter, take a derivative with respect to it, evaluate the integral and then integrate back to undo the derivative step. $$I_1(t)=\int_0^1 \frac{x^t-1}{\ln x}dx\Rightarrow I_1'(t)=\int_0^1x^tdx=\frac{x^{t+1}}{t+1}\bigg|_0^1 =\frac{1}{t+1}$$
